Building in public used to be risky. You might reveal your competitive edge, attract competitors, or share half-baked ideas that damage your credibility. Today, the calculus has changed.

Building in public is no longer a liability—it's a strategic advantage. Here's why.

## The Shift in Information Dynamics

In the industrial era, information was scarce. If you had it, you could protect it and gain an edge. Today, information is abundant—almost free. What's scarce now is *attention, trust, and authentic relationships*.

When you build in public, you're not just sharing information. You're building a community, demonstrating competence, and creating genuine engagement with people who care about what you're doing.

## Why Building in Public Works

### 1. **You Build an Audience, Not Just a Customer Base**
People buy from people they know and trust. By sharing your journey, you invite people to become invested in your success. They become advocates, not just customers.

### 2. **You Get Real Feedback Early**
Instead of spending months building in isolation, you expose your ideas to reality. You learn what people actually want, not what you assumed they wanted.

### 3. **You Remove Marketing Costs**
Traditional marketing is about reaching people and convincing them. Building in public attracts people who've already self-selected as interested in what you're doing. Your marketing becomes organic.

### 4. **You Demonstrate Competence**
Sharing your process, explaining your decisions, and being transparent about challenges shows competence in a way that polished sales pages never can.

## How to Build in Public

### Share Your Process
Document what you're building. Blog about decisions, post updates on social media, share challenges and how you're solving them.

### Be Honest About Progress
Don't fake success. People respect honesty. Sharing failures and learning from them builds more trust than only sharing wins.

### Engage With Your Audience
Building in public isn't a broadcast—it's a conversation. Reply to comments, answer questions, and genuinely care about the community you're building.

### Create Value Before Selling
Your early content shouldn't be sales pitches. Write helpful blogs, share insights, create tools or resources. Build authority first.

## The Risk of Building in Public

There are real risks: criticism, competitors watching, strategic leaks. But these risks are typically smaller than the upside.

The key is to share wisely. You don't need to share everything—share enough to build trust and demonstrate competence, but keep your core strategy private.

## Getting Started

Start small. Write one blog post about what you're building. Share your story on Twitter/X or LinkedIn. Reply thoughtfully to comments. Build gradually.

The companies and creators winning today aren't hiding—they're building in the open, inviting people along for the journey. The transparency is the moat, not the vulnerability.

What are you building? Consider sharing it.
